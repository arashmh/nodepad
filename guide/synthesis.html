Synthesis

Navigate

Synthesis is nodepad's most distinctive feature. The AI watches your notes accumulate and, when enough cross-category material exists, generates ghost notes — emergent insights that surface ideas you haven't explicitly stated: tensions, paradoxes, unexpected dependencies, or bridges between different topics.

Ghost notes are not summaries. The system is explicitly instructed to be additive — saying something the notes imply but do not state.

How to Open the Panel

  • Press Ctrl+K → select Synthesis
  • Click the Synthesis toggle in the status bar (badge shows count when ghost notes are available)
  • Close with the X button, Escape, or by opening a different panel

When Synthesis Triggers

Ghost generation is fully automatic — you never need to request it. It fires after each note is enriched (with a 2.5-second delay), but only when all of these conditions are met:

ConditionThreshold
Enriched notes in project≥ 5
Distinct categories present≥ 2
New notes since last generation≥ 5
Time since last generation≥ 5 minutes
Current ghost notes in panel< 5 (hard cap)

How Synthesis Works

The system builds a curated context window of up to 10 notes, biased toward recency and category diversity: it takes the 4 most recent notes, then fills in representatives from underrepresented categories. Previously generated ghost texts (up to 10) are sent back with an "AVOID" instruction, preventing near-duplicate syntheses.

What Each Ghost Note Shows

  • A category label — a one-word "bridge topic" naming the cross-cutting theme
  • The thesis text — a concise 15–25 word insight, question, or productive tension
  • While generating: a "Synthesizing..." animation with bouncing dots

Accepting or Dismissing

Add to canvas (✓ icon) — accepts the ghost note. It becomes a real thesis-type note on your canvas and goes through AI enrichment like any other note.

Dismiss (✕ icon) — removes the ghost note permanently without adding it to the canvas.

Empty State

When no ghost notes exist yet, the panel shows: "Emergent theses will appear here" with a sparkle icon. The footer reads: "Generated from your writing patterns".
